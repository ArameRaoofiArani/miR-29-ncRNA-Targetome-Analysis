Table S1. Candidate miR-29-associated genes used for correlation analysis. Genes were selected based on reported association with miR-29 from HMDD and literature sources and grouped into functional categories, including extracellular matrix remodeling, cell adhesion, growth factor signaling, inflammation, cell survival, and epigenetic regulation. These genes were used to assess relationships between candidate lncRNAs and miR-29-associated pathways in keratinocyte RNA-seq datasets.
Table S2. CLIP-based identification of candidate miR-29-associated noncoding RNAs (ncRNAs) in interfollicular keratinocytes. Enriched ncRNA transcripts identified from AGO-associated CLIP-seq data are shown. For each candidate, the gene name, Ensembl gene ID, normalized expression level (baseMean), and log2 fold change (CLIP vs input) are provided. All listed transcripts passed filtering criteria including expression threshold and enrichment (log2FC > 1), and were retained as candidate ncRNAs within the miR-29-associated targetome.
Figure S1. Interaction networks of miR-29a, miR-29b, and miR-29c with their associated target genes. Each network illustrates experimentally validated miR-29–target gene interactions, including genes involved in extracellular matrix remodeling, cell adhesion, growth factor signaling, inflammation, apoptosis, and epigenetic regulation. Nodes represent target genes, and edges indicate regulatory interactions.
